About Me

I am currently in my first year of my PhD in Computer Science at Unversity of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My broad research interests lies in the area of applying machine learning for an optimized system design. I am interested in building reliable and intelligent systems that are optimized in performance and can heal itself during any faults. Previously, I have worked in the fields of Data-driven Systems, Edge Computing, Distributed Systems and Federated Learning.

Previous to this, I have worked as a Research Associate 2 at Big Data Intelligence Lab, Adobe Research, India. I was a part of the 'Systems and Insights Group' where I primarily worked in the Systems area. My work at Adobe focused on varied projects. I have worked towards maintaining reliability of cloud serices by applying machine learning and causal techniques, as well as scheduling batched queries across different configuration of clusters, while I have also explored areas of using data for digital marketing experiences. Some of my projects have been successfully transferred to the engineering team and is being used in the products.

I graduated with a Dual Degree (Integrated B. Tech + M. Tech) in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT)Kharagpur. I was advised Prof. Sandip Chakraborty on my Master's Thesis on the topic of training machine learning models in a blockchain-based cross-silo federated network via blockchain interoperability and my bachelor's thesis project on the topic of viewport adaptive 360-degree video streaming.

Apart from my technical prowess, I am an avid reader (goodreads), sports enthusiast (especially football - P.S. a huge Barcelona and Messi fan), an adventure lover and a connoisseur of art and film-making. I like to read books and some of my favourite reads are 1984, To Kill a Mockingbird, Slaughterhouse-five and A Brief History of Time. In addition to that few of my favourite movies are The Shawshank Redemption, Parasite, Fight Club and The Godfather.
